Why The Unjournal?

Traditional academic publishing is slow, expensive, and misaligned with real-world impact. Researchers spend months reformatting papers. Peer review is unpaid, opaque, and produces binary accept/reject decisions. We built something better.

No Formatting Games

Submit your publicly hosted preprint, working paper, or any other research object, in whatever format you like. We evaluate the research, not the styling. What we look for.

Transparent Assessment

All evaluations are public, creating valuable metrics for authors, researchers, and funders. No more black-box peer review. See our evaluator guidelines.

Global Impact Focus

We prioritize research on global health, development, animal welfare, climate, and catastrophic risks. Evaluators assess reliability, robustness, and real-world usefulness.

Open Science

Supporting replicability and methodological rigor. We encourage dynamic documents, code integration, and transparent methods. Part of the broader open evaluation movement.

Living Research

Research evolves through feedback, new data, and refinements. We support living projects that aren't frozen in PDF form. Authors can respond publicly to evaluations.

Non-Exclusive

Having your work evaluated by us doesn't limit your options. Submit to any traditional journal before, during, or after our process. We don't claim publishing rights.

How We Compare

The Unjournal works alongside traditional publishing, not instead of it. Here's how our model differs:

Traditional Journals

  • Extensive formatting requirements
  • Anonymous, private peer review
  • Unpaid reviewers
  • Binary accept/reject decisions
  • Private revision cycles
  • Often exclusive submission
  • Months to years turnaround
  • Focus on novelty and "fit"

The Unjournal

  • No formatting — submit as-is
  • Public evaluations with DOIs
  • $300+ per evaluation + prizes
  • Quantitative ratings + feedback
  • Public, citable author responses
  • Completely non-exclusive
  • Weeks to months turnaround
  • Rigor, robustness, real-world relevance
