Why Submit Your Research?

Submitting to The Unjournal signals that you're confident in your work, open to constructive criticism, and motivated to seek the truth. It demonstrates engagement with the open science movement and connects your research with communities focused on global impact.

Non-exclusive: The Unjournal is completely non-exclusive. Having your paper evaluated here does not limit your options—you can still submit your work to any traditional journal before, during, or after our evaluation process.

We evaluate working papers, preprints, and dynamic documents. There are no formatting requirements. We assess the research itself, not how it's packaged.

Our focus: We evaluate quantitative research involving human behavior, markets, policy, and social outcomes—especially in economics, psychology, political science, and related fields. We prioritize work relevant to global health, development, animal welfare, environmental issues, AI governance, and catastrophic risks. We do not evaluate pure mathematics, natural sciences (biology, physics), or computer science/engineering research. See our full scope.

Key Benefits

💬

Substantive Expert Feedback

Quality feedback is hard to get, especially for early-career researchers. We connect you with paid evaluators who provide meaningful, detailed critique—not just surface-level comments.

🏆

Credibility & Recognition

Selection by The Unjournal reflects potential global relevance. Our quantitative ratings serve as credibility markers for funders, policymakers, and other researchers.

🗣

Public Response Opportunity

You can publicly respond to evaluations, clarifying misconceptions and correcting misunderstandings. Your response gets its own DOI and stands alongside the evaluations.

📢

Increased Visibility

We publicize evaluations through social media, forums, and longer-form content. Your work reaches the Open Science and Global Priorities communities.

🤝

Community Connection

Engagement opens doors to collaborations, grants, and networking with researchers focused on effective interventions and evidence-based policy.

💡

Innovation Recognition

Be recognized as an early adopter in open science. Authors who embrace transparent evaluation demonstrate commitment to research integrity.

💰

Potential Prize Eligibility

Highly-rated research may become eligible for our Impactful Research Prize, offering publicity, reputation, and potential financial awards (funding dependent).

💻

Format Flexibility

Unlike traditional journals, we encourage modern formats like Quarto, dynamic documents, and code-integrated research. Reproducibility is valued, not penalized.

Especially Valuable For

The Unjournal particularly helps researchers whose work may be:

  • Under-published due to time pressure or career transitions
  • Interdisciplinary and hard to place in traditional journals
  • Substantially improved post-publication
  • Strong empirically but undervalued by traditional academic incentives
  • In subfields with limited journal options

How It Works

1

Submit Your Work

Share a link to your publicly hosted work (ideally with a DOI). Use our submission form or email contact@unjournal.org.

2

Expert Evaluation

We engage two evaluators with relevant expertise. They provide detailed feedback and quantitative ratings across multiple dimensions.

3

Your Response

Before evaluations go public, you have two weeks to respond. Clarify doubts, explain your choices, highlight revisions.

4

Public Publication

Evaluations, ratings, and your response are published on unjournal.pubpub.org. Everything receives DOIs and is indexed in scholarly databases.

Light-touch requirements: Your work must be publicly hosted somewhere (e.g., SSRN, arXiv, OSF, personal website). A DOI is helpful but optional—we can help you get one. You may also suggest specific areas where you'd particularly like feedback. Code and data links are encouraged but not mandatory.

Frequently Asked Questions

What about negative feedback being public?

Unlike traditional anonymous peer review, our evaluations are public. But as Brian Nosek notes, to advance your career, you need people engaging critically with your work. Academics regularly receive critical feedback; public evaluation demonstrates confidence, not weakness.

We also offer conditional embargoes for early-career researchers concerned about visibility of evaluations during sensitive career stages.

Will this prevent me from publishing elsewhere?

No. The Unjournal is completely non-exclusive. You can submit to traditional journals before, during, or after our evaluation. We do not claim any publishing rights.

How should I respond to evaluations?

You don't need to address every point. Respond only where you have something substantive: clarifying doubts, explaining choices, highlighting revisions. Use clear narratives, group themes logically, and maintain a substantive tone.

If you're still revising, you can post a placeholder response noting that updates are forthcoming.

What if I update my paper after evaluation?

We prioritize evaluating the most recent version. Share substantial updates during evaluation—evaluators may revise their comments. We remain open to re-evaluating revised papers after initial publication if both authors and evaluators engage.

What about papers that weren't submitted?

The Unjournal sometimes selects papers directly for evaluation. If your work is chosen, you'll be notified and can request embargoes or specify desired feedback areas. You'll have two weeks to respond after evaluations are complete.

Can I request an embargo?

Yes. Email us at contact@unjournal.org or use the submission form to request a conditional embargo. Early-career researchers may receive exceptions for delayed publication of evaluations.
