The Unjournal is an open initiative. There are four ways to contribute — as an evaluator, a researcher, a field specialist or advisor, or a team member. All roles are remote and flexible.

Evaluate Research

Who we're looking for

Researchers at any career stage with expertise in quantitative social science, economics, global health, animal welfare, psychology, AI/tech policy, or related fields. PhD students and early-career researchers are welcome.

What's involved

You receive a paper in your area of expertise and provide:

  • A structured written evaluation (2–5 pages)
  • Numerical ratings on 7 criteria, each with confidence intervals

Most evaluations take 5–15 hours. Timelines are flexible and agreed in advance.

Compensation

Evaluators receive $250–$400 per completed evaluation, paid via Wise or PayPal. Your evaluation is published with a DOI on PubPub (ISSN 3071-2173) — a citable scholarly output that appears on your CV.

Current areas of particular need: Global health & development economics, animal welfare, catastrophic risks & AI safety, behavioral science, meta-science.

Evaluation criteria

Evaluators rate each paper on seven dimensions:

  • Overall assessment
  • Advancing knowledge and practice
  • Methods (justification, reasonableness, validity)
  • Logic and communication
  • Real-world relevance
  • Global priorities relevance
  • Open science practices

Submit or Recommend Research

There are two ways to bring research to The Unjournal: submit your own work for evaluation, or recommend a paper by someone else.

Submit your own work

You are an author who wants expert evaluation of your paper or preprint.

  • Free to submit; no publication fees
  • We prioritize papers in our research focus areas
  • Choose between confidential or public evaluation
  • Receive written feedback and numerical ratings from 2–3 evaluators
Submit your research

Recommend others' work

You know of a paper that deserves rigorous, open evaluation.

  • Recommend papers in our priority areas, including work by others
  • Recommendations from the research community help us identify impactful work
  • We consider all recommendations

Join the Team

The Unjournal is built on the contributions of researchers and specialists across many fields. We have roles suited to different levels of availability and expertise.

Field Specialist

Help identify and prioritize research in your area of expertise.

  • Nominate papers for evaluation
  • Review research priorities
  • Provide field context and guidance

Time commitment: low — approximately 2–5 hours per month typical. Currently voluntary (no compensation), though this may change.

Inquire about field specialist role

Research Affiliate / URA

Undergraduate, graduate, or early-career researchers assisting with research tasks.

  • Literature review and data analysis
  • Research synthesis
  • Evaluation management support

Flexible hours. May receive academic credit or stipend depending on arrangement and institutional context.

Inquire about URA/affiliate role

Advisory Board & Management

Senior researchers providing strategic guidance or operational leadership.

  • Advisory board roles filled by invitation based on field fit
  • Operational roles: remote, paid positions
  • Current opening: Operations Lead (~$30–55/hr)

Management roles are filled based on organizational need and fit.
