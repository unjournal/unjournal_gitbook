Why Support Us?

Every year, billions of dollars in philanthropic and policy decisions rely on academic research — yet this research is often poorly evaluated, stuck behind paywalls, or takes years to receive meaningful feedback. Traditional peer review is slow, secretive, and wastes researchers' time on journal-shopping rather than improving their work.

We're changing that. The Unjournal commissions fast, transparent, expert evaluation of research that matters — research informing decisions about global health, animal welfare, catastrophic risks, and evidence-based policy. Our evaluations are public, quantified, and useful.

We're helping transform academia

The academic journal system — built around printed journals and binary accept/reject decisions — is archaic. It discourages innovation, extracts rents, and creates a collective action problem that traps researchers in a system everyone knows is broken.

The Unjournal is helping coordinate an exit:

  • Demonstrating that journal-independent evaluation can provide credible, useful feedback
  • Encouraging a focus on impact — evaluating research that matters for real decisions
  • Boosting transparency and open science — public evaluations with quantified ratings
  • Building the track record and infrastructure for universities, funders, and researchers to adopt better systems
  • Exploring AI's potential for credible, reliable peer review

Your donation directly funds:

  • Expert evaluator compensation ($350–450 per evaluation)
  • Prizes for exceptional research and evaluation work
  • Our Pivotal Questions initiative — identifying high-value-of-information research questions
  • Infrastructure for open, journal-independent evaluation
  • Tool-building and AI research to understand how AI can support rigorous peer review

How we recognize your support

We believe in transparency — including about our own funding. We're happy to recognize your contribution in ways that match your preference:

  • On our PubPub evaluation pages: Donors at the $1,200+ level (sponsoring a full evaluation package) can be acknowledged as the sponsor of that specific evaluation, visible on the published page at unjournal.pubpub.org
  • Named evaluation series ($5,000+): A thematic series can carry your name or your organization's — e.g., "The [Your Name] Series on Climate Adaptation Research"
  • In our annual reports and communications: With your permission, we acknowledge funders in our reports to the community, on the EA Forum, and in presentations
  • Larger contributions: For major gifts, we're happy to discuss recognition on unjournal.org, our interactive dashboard, and other venues — let's talk about what makes sense
  • Anonymous giving: We also welcome anonymous donations — just let us know your preference

Open Science Advocates

Help us fix academic publishing

The journal system is broken: slow, expensive, secretive, creating perverse incentives. The Unjournal offers a working alternative:

  • Public evaluations with accountability
  • Quantified ratings enabling comparison
  • Dynamic documents and living research (not frozen PDFs)
  • No paywalls, no article processing charges

We're also exploring AI's potential for supporting peer review — understanding where LLMs can help and where human expertise remains essential.

Event & Workshop Sponsors

Sponsor events that bring researchers and practitioners together

We're building a community around better research evaluation. Your support can help fund:

  • In-person evaluation workshops — hands-on sessions where researchers receive and discuss evaluations of their work in real time
  • Joint workshops with i4replication.org — combining replication research and open evaluation for maximum credibility
  • Online public seminars — researcher/practitioner sessions with shared feedback, bringing evaluators, authors, and policy audiences together
  • Pivotal Questions workshops — focused sessions with organizations and policymakers to identify the open empirical questions that matter most for impact

Event sponsorship includes recognition in all materials and communications. Let's discuss what would be most valuable for your organization.

Individual Researchers

Invest in the evaluation system you wish existed

You know the problems: months gaming journals, opaque feedback, desk rejections. We're building something better:

  • Fair compensation for evaluation work (unlike volunteer peer review)
  • Recognition and prizes for rigorous, useful evaluations
  • Infrastructure for evaluating dynamic documents and code
  • Research on AI-assisted evaluation to improve peer review quality

Even small donations help — and signal to funders and institutions that researchers want reform.
