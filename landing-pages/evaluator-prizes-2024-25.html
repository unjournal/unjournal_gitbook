We are proud to announce the winners of The Unjournal's 2024–25 Evaluator Prize. These prizes recognize evaluators whose work exemplifies the kind of open, rigorous, and constructive evaluation that drives better research and more informed policy.

The Unjournal commissions expert evaluation of quantitative research informing global priorities—in areas such as global health, development economics, animal welfare, climate policy, and existential risk. Our evaluators provide detailed feedback, quantitative ratings across multiple dimensions, and considered assessments of each paper's strengths and limitations. The best evaluations go beyond traditional peer review: they engage deeply with the research methods, consider real-world implications, offer specific and actionable suggestions, and communicate their findings clearly to diverse audiences.

This year, we allocated $6,500 in evaluator prizes across four prize tiers, selected through a process of evaluation manager ratings, team voting, and advisory board input.

Why We Award Evaluator Prizes

The Unjournal believes that peer review is among the most important—and most undervalued—activities in academic research. Traditional journals rely on unpaid, anonymous review, offering reviewers little recognition or incentive beyond a sense of professional duty. This creates perverse incentives: reviews tend to be rushed, superficial, or strategic rather than genuinely constructive.

We take a different approach. The Unjournal compensates all evaluators for their time and expertise, and we go further by awarding substantial prizes for exceptional work. Our goals are to:

  • Reward quality. Evaluators who invest significant effort, demonstrate deep expertise, and provide genuinely useful feedback deserve recognition.
  • Set visible benchmarks. By highlighting outstanding evaluations publicly, we help establish what excellent peer review looks like—creating models for future evaluators.
  • Signal that evaluation is valuable work. Prize-winning evaluations become part of an evaluator's professional portfolio, citable and publicly attributed (for those who choose to be named).
  • Improve research that matters. The research we evaluate addresses questions relevant to global health, development, environmental sustainability, and other areas where better evidence can save and improve lives. Stronger evaluation leads to more reliable evidence.
Our commitment: For the 2024–25 cycle, The Unjournal set aside $75 per evaluation toward prize incentives, building a pool of $6,500 in evaluator prizes. Combined with base compensation ($300+ per evaluation) and promptness bonuses, our average evaluator compensation target is approximately $450 per evaluation.

How Winners Were Selected

Prize recipients were selected through a multi-step process involving evaluation managers, the broader Unjournal team, and advisory board members. The process included internal rating by evaluation managers, discussion of nominated evaluations, and a team-wide vote.

Depth & Rigor

How deeply did the evaluation engage with the paper's methods, assumptions, and evidence? Did it go beyond surface-level review to provide technical substance?

💡

Constructive Insight

Did the evaluation offer specific, actionable suggestions? Did it identify ways to strengthen the work rather than simply cataloging weaknesses?

🌎

Real-World Relevance

Did the evaluation consider the paper's implications for policy, practice, or global priorities? Did it help readers assess how much to rely on the findings?

📝

Communication

Was the evaluation clearly written and accessible? Could it be useful to researchers, practitioners, and policymakers—not just narrow specialists?

🔁

Follow-through

Did the evaluator engage in iterative dialogue—responding to manager queries, collaborating with co-evaluators, or providing additional analysis when needed?

⚖

Expertise

Did the evaluation demonstrate genuine subject-matter expertise and the ability to assess claims critically within the relevant field?

These criteria align with The Unjournal's evaluator guidelines, which ask evaluators to provide substantive evaluation (resembling a high-quality journal referee report, without the binary accept/reject focus), quantitative ratings across nine dimensions with uncertainty ranges, and considered assessments of each paper's main claims.

About The Unjournal

The Unjournal commissions rigorous, public peer evaluation of quantitative research—especially in economics, policy, and social science—that informs global priorities and the most effective interventions for improving lives.

We don't “publish” papers in the traditional sense. Instead, we provide journal-independent evaluation: expert assessments with quantitative ratings and detailed feedback, all made publicly available. Authors retain full control of their work and can submit it anywhere else.

Our evaluators rate papers on multiple dimensions—including overall quality, strength of evidence, methodological rigor, contribution to knowledge and practice, and relevance to global priorities—using percentile rankings with explicit uncertainty ranges. This approach provides far richer information than a binary accept/reject decision, helping readers, funders, and policymakers understand exactly how much confidence to place in any given finding.
