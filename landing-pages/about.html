In a Nutshell

We are not a journal.

The Unjournal is an independent, nonprofit organization that coordinates expert public evaluation of research papers and projects. We publish evaluations, quantitative ratings, and author responses openly—creating transparent credibility signals for funders, policymakers, and other researchers.

We specifically target quantitative work that informs global priorities in economics, social science, and policy. Unlike traditional journals, researchers can submit work to us while simultaneously pursuing traditional publication. We charge no fees and compensate evaluators for their time.

What We Do

🔎

Commission Evaluations

We identify and select high-impact research, then engage qualified experts to provide rigorous, detailed assessments.

📈

Quantitative Ratings

Evaluators provide ratings across multiple dimensions: overall quality, methods, relevance, and communication—with confidence bounds.

📖

Publish Everything

Evaluations, ratings, and author responses are published on PubPub with DOIs, indexed in scholarly databases.

Our platform: All evaluations are publicly available at unjournal.pubpub.org. Explore our ratings and data through our interactive dashboard.

How We're Different

Not a journal. Something better.

Non-exclusive: Submit to us and traditional journals simultaneously

✓

✓

✓

Author responses: Public dialogue, not private revision cycles

✓

Impact focus: We prioritize real-world relevance over novelty

Operating as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit, The Unjournal is funded by organizations including the Effective Altruism Infrastructure Fund and Long-Term Future Fund. We charge no submission fees. All compensation to evaluators and prizes for quality work come from grants.

Research Areas

We focus on quantitative research informing global priorities, especially in these areas:

Global Health Development Economics Animal Welfare Climate & Environment Catastrophic Risk Institutional Reform AI Governance Technology & Society Evidence-Based Policy Charitable Giving Forecasting Meta-Science

We're particularly interested in research that could inform high-stakes decisions by funders, policymakers, and organizations working on pressing global challenges. See our prioritization guidelines for more detail.
