# DALY/QALY↔WELLBY Conversion

**Duration:** 25 minutes
**Speaker:** Julian Jamison (University of Exeter)

---

## Overview

Approaches to interconvertibility between health measures (DALYs/QALYs) and wellbeing measures (WELLBYs). This is crucial for funders comparing physical health vs mental health interventions.

---

## The Core Challenge

Funders like GiveWell use DALYs; mental health researchers often use WELLBYs. How do we compare:
- Malaria bed nets (measured in DALYs averted)
- StrongMinds therapy (measured in WELLBYs gained)

---

## Discussion Prompts

1. **What conversion factor should funders use?** (1 DALY = X WELLBYs)

2. **Is SD-equivalence defensible?** (1 SD WELLBY ≈ 1 SD DALY)

3. **What's lost from current rough conversion approaches?**

---

## Current Approaches

| Approach | Description | Pros | Cons |
|----------|-------------|------|------|
| Direct conversion | 1 DALY = 6 WELLBYs | Simple | Arbitrary |
| SD equivalence | 1 SD ≈ 1 SD | Statistically principled | Assumes similar distributions |
| Hybrid | Context-dependent | Flexible | Complex |

## Related Pivotal Questions

- **DALY_03**: What mapping structure/approach should we use (direct units, SDs, other)?
