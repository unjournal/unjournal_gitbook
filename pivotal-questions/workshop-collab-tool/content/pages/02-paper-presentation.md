# Paper Presentation: Benjamin et al.

**Duration:** 25 minutes
**Speakers:** Dan Benjamin (UCLA/NBER), Miles Kimball (University of Colorado Boulder)

---

## Overview

Scale-use heterogeneity findings and calibration methods from the paper "Adjusting for Scale-Use Heterogeneity in Self-Reported Well-Being."

### Paper Summary

The paper addresses how different people use subjective scales differently, potentially biasing wellbeing comparisons across groups or interventions.

**Key findings:**
- Scale-use heterogeneity is significant and measurable
- Calibration methods can adjust for these differences
- Adjustments may change intervention rankings

---
