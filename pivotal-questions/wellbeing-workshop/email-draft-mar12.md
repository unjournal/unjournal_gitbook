## What we're doing and why

This is a new approach, and our first pilot. We're bringing together researchers, practitioners, and funders to focus on two questions that matter for real funding decisions:

1. How reliable are linear WELLBYs (relative to alternatives) for comparing LMIC interventions?
2. How should we convert between DALYs/QALYs and WELLBYs?

The goals: help researchers understand practitioners' highest-value questions and trade-offs; help practitioners understand the most relevant up-to-date research; get on the same page on terminology and key cruxes; and measure our beliefs openly with calibrated uncertainty to drive high-value-of-information updating. Ultimately, we hope this leads to better decisions about measuring and comparing the impact of interventions in LMICs.

## Epistemic ground rules

We're aiming for especially good discussion norms: modesty, charitable interpretation, reasoning transparency, calibrated uncertainty, and tracking the crux. For each issue, consider: *what evidence could change my mind?* Let's focus on the highest-value-of-information questions — where will our discussion yield the most value?
