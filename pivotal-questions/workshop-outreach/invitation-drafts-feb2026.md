## 2. Joel McGuire (HLI — Research Director)

**Subject:** Invitation: WELLBY measurement workshop — HLI's perspective is essential

Hi Joel,

The Unjournal is organizing an online workshop on wellbeing measurement, and HLI's participation is essential. You've done more applied work with WELLBYs than almost anyone, and the questions we're tackling directly affect how HLI's cost-effectiveness estimates should be interpreted.

The workshop (mid-March 2026, ~3.5 hours total with scheduled segments) brings together:

- Benjamin et al. (scale-use heterogeneity paper authors)
- Founders Pledge (who raised these questions)
- The Unjournal evaluators (Kaiser, Prati)
- Other practitioners and researchers

The core questions:

1. **Is the linear WELLBY reliable enough** for comparing cost-effectiveness across mental health, physical health, and consumption interventions?
2. **How should we convert between DALYs and WELLBYs** — and how sensitive are funding decisions to the conversion approach?

Your experience with HLI's CEA methodology would be particularly valuable in the practitioner panel — discussing how funders should navigate DALY/WELLBY comparison now, and what research would change minds.

We're also doing a beliefs elicitation exercise where participants state priors on specific operationalized questions — creating a baseline for future updating. Your calibrated estimates would be valuable input.

Would anyone else at HLI want to join?
