## EA Forum Quick Take

**Title**: Workshop: Can WELLBYs Replace DALYs for Cross-Cause Comparison? (March 16, limited spots)

---

**The Unjournal** is hosting a virtual expert workshop on **Monday, March 16, 2026 (11am-5pm ET)** focused on two pivotal questions for effective giving:

1. **How reliable are WELLBY-based evaluations** of interventions targeting subjective wellbeing?
2. **Can we convert between DALYs/QALYs and WELLBYs** -- and if so, at what exchange rate?

These questions matter directly for cross-cause comparison. If funders like GiveWell, Open Philanthropy, or Founders Pledge want to compare a mental health program (measured in WELLBYs) against a malaria bednet program (measured in DALYs), they need answers here.

**Confirmed participants include:**

- **Dan Benjamin** (UCLA/NBER) & **Miles Kimball** (CU Boulder) -- presenting their foundational work on wellbeing measurement
- **Julian Jamison** (U of Exeter) -- presenting on DALY-WELLBY conversion
- **Caspar Kaiser** (U of Warwick) -- on barriers to WELLBY validity (comparability, linearity, neutral point)
- **Christian Krekel** (LSE), **Daniel Rogger** (World Bank), **Anthony Lepinteur** (U of Luxembourg)
- **Peter Hickman** (Coefficient Giving) & **Matt Lerner** (Founders Pledge) -- practitioner/funder perspectives

**Format**: ~3.5 hours of live sessions across a 6-hour window. Drop in for segments of interest. Segments cover stakeholder framing, paper presentation, evaluator discussion, WELLBY reliability, DALY conversion, beliefs elicitation, and a practitioner panel. Async participation is available for those who can't attend live.

**We're particularly looking for**: policymakers, practitioners at funding organizations, and researchers working on health/wellbeing measurement -- especially anyone involved in NICE methods, GBD/IHME disability weights, or cross-cause cost-effectiveness.

**Spots are limited** because we want active, engaged discussion (not a passive webinar). If you're interested, please fill out the **[interest and availability form](https://uj-wellbeing-workshop.netlify.app/interest)**.

More about the workshop: [uj-wellbeing-workshop.netlify.app/about](https://uj-wellbeing-workshop.netlify.app/about)

## LinkedIn Post

Can we compare the value of a mental health intervention to a malaria bednet? That requires bridging two measurement frameworks -- DALYs and WELLBYs -- and the evidence base is still thin.

On March 16, The Unjournal is convening researchers and practitioners to tackle this head-on in a virtual workshop:

-> How reliable are WELLBY-based evaluations?
-> Can we meaningfully convert between DALYs and WELLBYs?

Participants include Dan Benjamin (UCLA), Julian Jamison (Exeter), Caspar Kaiser (Warwick), Christian Krekel (LSE), Daniel Rogger (World Bank), and practitioners from Founders Pledge and Coefficient Giving.

We have a few remaining spots and are especially keen to hear from:

- Policymakers working with health economic evaluation (NICE, WHO, national HTA bodies)
- Practitioners at funding organizations doing cross-cause comparison
- Researchers in wellbeing measurement, disability weights, or QALYs

This isn't a passive webinar -- we want engaged discussion. Limited spots.
