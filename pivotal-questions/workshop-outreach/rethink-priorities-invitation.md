Hi John and Aisling,

The Unjournal is hosting a modular online workshop on **Monday, March 16** (11am–4pm ET / 3–8pm UK) focused on two practical measurement questions:

1. How reliable are linear WELLBYs for comparing interventions in low-income settings?
2. How should funders convert between DALYs/QALYs and WELLBYs?

These come up whenever organizations try to compare health interventions (measured in DALYs) against mental health or economic interventions (measured in WELLBYs). I think RP's GH&D team would find parts of this relevant — especially the DALY-WELLBY conversion discussion and the practitioner panel on practical implications for funders.

**Confirmed participants** include Dan Benjamin (UCLA/NBER, presenting on scale-use heterogeneity), Julian Jamison (Exeter, presenting on DALY-WELLBY conversion), Caspar Kaiser (Warwick), Christian Krekel (LSE), Daniel Rogger (World Bank), and practitioners from Founders Pledge and Coefficient Giving.

The format is modular — ~3.5 hours of sessions across a 5-hour window, so you can drop in for just the segments that interest you. No preparation required for most segments.
