## Belief Elicitation Questions (Draft)

These draw from your existing operationalized PQs. Participants would state their credences (0-100%) or point estimates before the workshop discussion, creating a recorded baseline.

### Question 1: Linear WELLBY Reliability
"Suppose a large, well-powered RCT finds that Intervention A improves average life satisfaction (0-10 Cantril ladder) by 0.5 points for 1 year, and Intervention B averts 0.1 DALYs per person. Using the linear WELLBY framework (where 1 WELLBY = 1 point on the 0-10 scale for 1 year), what is your credence (0-100%) that the linear WELLBY ranking of these interventions would agree with the ranking from an ideal welfare measure?"

### Question 2: Scale-Use Heterogeneity Importance
"How much would adjusting for scale-use heterogeneity (as in Benjamin et al.) change the relative cost-effectiveness ranking of the top 10 interventions currently compared using WELLBYs? (1 = no change in any rankings, 5 = would substantially change the top 3)"

### Question 3: DALY-WELLBY Conversion
"What is your credence (0-100%) that comparing interventions by their standard-deviation improvements (1 SD WELLBY ≈ 1 SD DALY) introduces less than 20% bias relative to an ideal conversion based on person-tradeoff surveys?"

### Question 4: Measurement Improvement Value
"If funders added calibration questions (à la Benjamin et al.) and stated-preference tradeoff questions to 5 major intervention RCTs over the next 3 years, what is your credence (0-100%) that this would substantially change (by >15%) the relative cost-effectiveness estimate for at least one of the top-5 funded interventions?"

### Question 5: Better Alternatives
"What is your credence (0-100%) that there exists a practically implementable wellbeing metric that would outperform the linear WELLBY for cross-intervention cost-effectiveness comparison, if widely adopted within 5 years?"

*These can be refined — the key is having specific, quantifiable questions that create a useful baseline for the PQ evaluation project.*
