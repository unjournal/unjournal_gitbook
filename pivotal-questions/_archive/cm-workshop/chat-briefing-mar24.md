I'm David Reinstein, director of The Unjournal. I'm planning an online workshop on cultivated meat cost trajectories and animal welfare funding implications, targeting late April / early May 2026 (~2 hours). This is part of our Pivotal Questions initiative.

## The Focal Question (CM_01)

What will be the average production cost (per edible kg) of cultured chicken meat in 2031, 2036, and 2051, across all large-scale plants (>2kt/yr)?

Defined as: (Annualized capital charge + all operating costs) ÷ annual kg output, in 2025 USD. Excludes R&D, regulatory, distribution, marketing.

Related questions:
- CM_10/11: Will $100K donated to CM development yield more animal welfare benefit than the same to The Humane League's corporate campaigns?
- CM_12-20: Subquestions on hydrolysates, growth factors, media costs, bioreactor design, cell density

## Emerging Cruxes / Themes

Based on the research pipeline, email correspondence, and evaluator feedback, these seem to be the key disagreements:

### 1. Media costs: Are they actually coming down fast enough?
- Humbird's amino acid prices were 2-10x too high (per GFI's real supplier quotes)
- But even corrected, media remains the largest variable cost
- Hydrolysate adoption (CM_12) could be transformative — cheap plant-derived replacement for expensive purified growth factors
- GFI amino acid report (Dec 2025) is the newest hard data here

### 2. The Pasitka paradox: Great TEA, dead company
- Published $6/lb continuous production in Nature Food
- Company (Believer Meats) shut down Dec 2025
- Gap between TEA projections under ideal conditions vs commercial reality
- Does this discredit the science or just the business execution?

### 3. Bioreactor scale-up: Custom vs pharma
- CM_20: Will companies build their own bioreactors (much cheaper) or use pharma-grade?
- Vow (Australia) claims to have built 20k-liter bioreactor for <$1M in 14 weeks
- Humbird assumed pharma-grade (expensive); industry is moving to food-grade
- Cell density achievable at scale (CM_16) is a key bottleneck

### 4. The animal welfare funding question
- CM_10/11: Is $100K for CM development better than $100K for corporate campaigns (THL)?
- This depends on cost trajectory beliefs AND probability of adoption AND timeline
- Funders (Open Phil, ACE) need this answered — it's the decision-relevant question
- Even optimistic cost projections don't guarantee animal welfare impact if adoption is slow

### 5. Environmental claims: Asset or liability?
- Risner & Spang found CM could be 4-25x worse than beef for GWP under pharma-grade scenarios
- GFI pushed back hard (formal critique letter)
- If CM can't claim environmental benefits, the case rests entirely on animal welfare + taste/price
- Food-grade vs pharma-grade media is the swing factor here too

### 6. The "cell ag winter" and what comes next
- Isha Datar (New Harvest): "trough of disillusionment," need "new scientific milestones" not more money
- Multiple companies have failed or scaled back
- Ricardo San Martin (Berkeley): without demonstrated cell density improvements, "it's speculation"
- But Lever VC claims a "second generation" is breaking through cost barriers

## Lessons from the Wellbeing Workshop (what NOT to do)

Survey feedback from 5+ respondents:
- **Dan Benjamin**: "a bit overwhelming, especially receiving it all so close to the start" and "a bit overwhelming to have links to so many novel tools." Wanted "Less prereading."
- **Peter Hickman**: "It was long and I didn't finish it. It felt hard to parse all the info." Wanted more structure.
- **Michael Plant** (HLI): Called it "overengineered." Preferred "standard academic format."
- **Yaniv Reingewertz**: "quite a lot of text"

Key takeaways for CM workshop:
- Fewer tools (don't ask people to use Hypothes.is + Google Docs + Coda forms + Metaculus + NotebookLM)
- Send materials well in advance, not all at once
- Shorter, clearer beliefs form
- More structure — people should know exactly what's happening when
- Keep it to ~2 hours maximum
- Don't try to cover everything — pick the highest-value discussions
