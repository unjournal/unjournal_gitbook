## Post body

The Unjournal is organizing a small-group online workshop on the cost trajectory of cultivated meat — and what it means for animal welfare funding decisions.

**What will cultivated meat actually cost to produce?** The answer matters enormously. If costs fall close to conventional meat, CM could eventually spare billions of animals. If costs remain high, the hundreds of millions in AW funding directed at CM may be poorly allocated relative to proven interventions like corporate campaigns.

New techno-economic analyses (TEAs) have widened the range of estimates dramatically — from Pasitka et al.'s claim of ~$6/lb under continuous production, to Humbird's conclusion that cost parity is "highly unlikely" given biological and engineering constraints. Meanwhile, Believer Meats (the company behind the optimistic Pasitka TEA) shut down in December 2025, adding a real-world data point to the debate.

We're bringing together a small group of TEA researchers, evaluators, and animal welfare funders/stakeholders to work through this evidence together, using structured beliefs elicitation on operationalized questions. This follows our [Wellbeing Measurement workshop](https://uj-wellbeing-workshop.netlify.app/about.html) (March 2026), which used this format productively.

### Core questions

1. **What does the TEA evidence tell us?** Optimistic vs pessimistic analyses — what explains the gap? Which assumptions are most contested?
2. **What will CM cost in 2031, 2036, and 2051?** Structured expert beliefs on [CM_01](https://www.metaculus.com/c/unjournal/38815/) (average production cost per edible kg). Key cruxes: media costs, growth factor pricing, hydrolysate adoption, bioreactor scale, food-grade vs pharmaceutical-grade inputs.
3. **How does CM compare to proven AW interventions?** [CM_10/11](https://www.metaculus.com/c/unjournal/40925/): Would $100K for CM development yield more animal welfare benefit than $100K for The Humane League's corporate campaigns?
4. **What should funders do now?** What evidence would change the calculus? What research has the highest value of information?

Many of these cruxes cut across the questions — cell density drives media costs, which drive production cost, which drives the funding comparison.

### Format

- **~2 hours of live sessions**, fully online, in modular segments — you can join only the parts you're interested in
- **Asynchronous participation** — submit beliefs and comments before or after the live event
- **Structured beliefs elicitation** on our [operationalized pivotal questions](https://uj-cm-workshop.netlify.app/beliefs.html) (CM_01–CM_20), several of which are live on [Metaculus](https://www.metaculus.com/c/unjournal/)
- We plan to record and share transcripts (with participant consent)

### Proposed segments

- **Stakeholder Context** (~15 min): Why CM cost matters for AW funding decisions
- **TEA Evidence Review** (~30 min): Landscape of analyses, evaluator findings, key disagreements
- **Cost Trajectory Discussion + Beliefs** (~35 min): Expert discussion on CM_01, subquestions on media, growth factors, bioreactors
- **Animal Welfare ROI & Practitioner Panel** (~30 min): CM funding vs alternatives, funder perspectives

### Who we're looking for

We're aiming for a focused discussion among people deeply involved in this question:

- **TEA researchers and modelers** (cost modeling, bioprocess engineering, cell culture scale-up)
- **Animal welfare funders and practitioners** making allocation decisions that involve CM
- **Forecasters** with experience in technology cost trajectories or animal welfare metrics
- **Evaluators** with expertise in assessing techno-economic evidence

This is a small-group workshop, not a webinar — we want substantive interaction. **Anyone is welcome to express interest**; we'll invite a core discussion group and provide participation opportunities (async contributions, beliefs elicitation, annotating materials) for a broader set of contributors.
