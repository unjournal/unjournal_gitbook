# WELLBY Reliability Discussion

**Duration:** 25 minutes

---

## Overview

Is the linear WELLBY reliable enough for cross-intervention comparison? This segment dives deep into the foundational questions about WELLBY as a measurement tool.

---

## Key Questions for Discussion

### Cardinality
Is it defensible to treat the difference between 3→4 the same as 7→8 on a life satisfaction scale?

### Neutral Point
Where is the neutral point on life satisfaction scales? Is 5/10 truly neutral, or does it represent mild dissatisfaction?

### Dimensionality
How much insight is lost by collapsing multiple wellbeing dimensions into a single number?

### Life Satisfaction vs Experience
Should we rely on evaluative measures (life satisfaction) or experiential measures (moment-to-moment affect)?

---

## Discussion Prompts

1. **Is cardinality defensible** (3→4 vs 7→8)?

2. **Where is the neutral point** on life satisfaction scales?

3. **How much insight is lost** vs other wellbeing measures?

4. **Should we use life satisfaction or experience measures?**

---

## Related Pivotal Questions

- **WELL_01**: How reliable is the WELLBY relative to other wellbeing measures?
- **WELL_04**: Is it better to use a single WELLBY measure or measure dimensions separately?
- **WELL_07**: What is lost by using linear WELLBY instead of more complex measures?
- **WELL_08**: Should the metric be based on life satisfaction or experience measures?
- **WELL_09**: If relying on Cantril ladder, how best to convert to welfare metric?
